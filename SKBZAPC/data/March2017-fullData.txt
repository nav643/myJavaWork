-------
The Alumni Chipped In Just When It Was Needed 

SKBZAPS had its Irtiqa'a School Inspection from 20th February 2017 to 23rd February 2017. Parallel to ADEC's Irtiqa'a Inspection, the OSHAD i.e. Occupational Safety and Health inspection was also taking place. Both these inspections were extremely crucial this time because the existence and survival of the school was at stake. 

The almost dilapidated condition of various parts of the school building and its failing water supply system are a crystal clear evidence of the negligence of those individuals or bodies that were responsible for the school's good upkeep and maintenance, no matter what claims are made by them.

Schools need to be loved and respected just as a person who loves and maintains his car finds that it lives longer and remains healthier. Similarly, if well brought up grown-ups love, respect and look after after their aging parents, the parents stay healthy and happy.  So, the living and the non-living both need loving care for good health and longevity. Lip service only doesn’t move the needle.


The failures of school water supply system were an embarrassment on normal working days but could be FATAL during the OSHAD Inspection. That's where the Alumni chipped in and donated two brand new pumps with automatic control panels to replace the unreliable makeshift water pumping system.

The school  owes the founding office bearers of the Alumni Association a huge debt of gratitude for their timely contribution towards the health of their aging Alma Mater. 

It's an incontrovertible fact that nobody cares more than the old students for their Alma Mater. The faculty and the Principal  are doing their best to upgrade the basic function of the school i. e. teaching and learning. But more hands and resources are required to bring our school up to par. 

Our next infrastructural challenges are the provision of quality waterproofing of the school roof, renovation of all toilets along with toilets for the handicapped, removing sharp edges and providing slopes, the big ticket challenge of installing a lift for the SEN students, provision of more interactive smart boards, and greening of the football ground of the school.

Looking forward to the good days ahead for the school, about which there is not a sliver of doubt in my mind, because of the founding of the  Alumni Association.

Principal 
SKBZAPS 
Abdul Qudus Saggu                     dated: 3rd March 2017
